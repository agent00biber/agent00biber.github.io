About me

I am a scientist by heart. I always enjoyed the deductive part of a problem and finding a simple solution to it. Now, I am also a scientist by diploma. In mid-2017 I graduated as Master of Science from the Albert Ludwig University of Freiburg in ‘pharmaceutical sciences’. At this moment, I’m looking to start my career and do the thing I love and am capable of: to solve problems.

There is another part of me who likes to create. At first, I just wanted to have my own website for a better way to present myself to future employers but now it is much more for me — It is fun. After a rough start, I was able to create the designs I wanted and learned so much every day. For me, it was a puzzle worth solving and therefore I had the motivation to try again every day.

As of now, I think of myself as a
scientist and a web designer.

My first website. It was very static and featured a parallax effect, which was easy to implement. It was picture-heavy, slow loading and still close to a text document.

The second iteration. For me, this was very artsy at the time but impractical and not easy to scan for information. I started to use motion and javascript (jquery and onScoll libraries like waypoints). I also abandoned bootstrap for CSS grid as the primary layout framework.

The double scroll website was a desktop-first website. It only worked on a screen, which was wider than tall. So I made a different website for mobile, which featured even more motion. I had difficulties to make it work on every height because I designed it like an app.

For this website, I prepared better. I researched, designed and prototyped it beforehand and not while coding. I got into UX-Design, Information Architecture and typography for a simpler, timeless approach.
